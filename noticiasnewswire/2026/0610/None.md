# Pix 11 and 21 Events Present an Exciting Live Celebration of Latin Music at The 116th Street Festival June 13th 1-5

NEW YORK, NY — June 10, 2026 — (NOTICIAS NEWSWIRE) — PIX11, in association with 21 Events, proudly presents a dynamic day of music, culture, and community at the iconic 116th Street Festival in East Harlem. The PIX11 Stage will offer an extraordinary lineup of legendary performers, rising stars, and special tributes that celebrates the rich musical heritage of the Latino community while introducing audiences to the next generation of artists, all carried live by the television station.

 One of the highlights of this year’s program will be a special tribute to salsa icon Willie Colón, featuring performances by Luis Damon and Papote Jiménez. The tribute will culminate with a special appearance by Grammy Award-winning artist Roberto Blades performing the classic anthem “La Murga,” honoring one of the most influential figures in Latin music history.

 The PIX11 Stage will also welcome Tito Puente Jr., who continues to carry forward the legacy of his legendary father while introducing new generations to the rhythms and energy that made the Puente name synonymous with Latin music worldwide.

 Adding to the excitement, freestyle and Latin music icon Safire will return to her roots for a truly special moment. Raised in Spanish Harlem just blocks from the festival grounds, this marks the first time she will perform in the neighborhood where her musical journey began. Safire is also preparing for the release of her highly anticipated Spanish-language album in August 2026.

 “As one of New York City’s most celebrated cultural events, the 116th Street Festival continues to serve as a gathering place where music, heritage, and community come together in the heart of El Barrio and we at 11 are proud to broadcast this excitement to fans throughout the Tri-State area,” said Michael Schwartz, Director of Sales for PIX11.

 The PIX11 Stage reflects the diversity, innovation, and enduring spirit of Latin music, bringing together artists whose careers span multiple generations, genres, and cultures.

 Internationally acclaimed violinist Alfredo De La Fe will bring his signature Violin sound to the festival as he celebrates his 60th anniversary in music. Widely recognized as the first solo violinist to perform with a salsa orchestra, Alfredo has performed alongside some of the greatest names in Latin music, including Celia Cruz, Tito Puente, Willie Colón, Héctor Lavoe, Eddie Palmieri, and the Fania All-Stars.

 The celebration will also feature performances by Colombian singer-songwriter Yuri Toro, former vocalist of Grupo Niche; veteran salsa vocalist Frankie Morales; Panama’s Willie Panamá, known as “The King of Salsa Rap”; New York-based salsa project Fuego Taibori featuring lead vocalist David Sanchez; and emerging Bronx-based duo CRILL & CLOS, who represent a new generation of bilingual artists blending Latin, pop, and urban influences.

 “For the 21 Events Team, we are honored to bring together so many talented artists to present their music to the live audience at the 122 Street Stage and to broadcast the 41st Annual 116 Street Festival live on PIX 11,” said Ralph Paniagua, Managing Director of 21 Events.

 The PIX11 Stage is produced by Ralph Paniagua, with musical production by Luis Miguel Cruz, co-production and music licensing by Martha Cancel, stage management by Pioantonio Ventura, and artist relations coordinated by Manuel Soba.

 Presented by PIX11 in association with 21 Events.
