# Hispanic Target Magazine &amp; Top Entrepreneur Awards 2025

MIAMI, FL — December 18, 2025 — (NOTICIAS NEWSWIRE) — Hispanic Target Magazine proudly hosted the Top Entrepreneur Awards 2025, a landmark event held during Art Basel Miami, aligning with the official Miami-Dade County cultural agenda through the Hispanic Affairs Advisory Board (HAAB). The celebration honored outstanding entrepreneurs, artists, and political leaders whose work has significantly contributed to the social, cultural, and economic development of South Florida’s Hispanic community.

 Set against the vibrant international backdrop of Art Basel, the event positioned Hispanic Target Magazine as a leading platform for recognizing Hispanic excellence across industries. The evening blended art, leadership, entrepreneurship, and cultural diplomacy, creating a space where influence and purpose converged.

 The ceremony was hosted in collaboration with TOP 67 Art Gallery, represented by acclaimed visual artist Adriana Torres, and was presented by Nancy Clara, Editorial Director of Hispanic Target Magazine, whose presence reinforced the publication’s mission to spotlight transformative Hispanic voices.

 Honoring Leadership and Impact

 The Top Entrepreneur Awards 2025 recognized individuals whose professional trajectories reflect innovation, resilience, and a commitment to community building. Award recipients included:

 * Marcos Acuña, CEO of Quick Scan & Go, for innovation in technology-driven consumer solutions
* Dr. Griselle Marino, Director of the Hispanic Affairs Advisory Board of Miami-Dade County, for public service and advocacy
* Claudia Serrer, Commissioner of ProChile Miami, for economic diplomacy and international trade leadership
* Adriana Bianco, Director of the Argentine and Latin American Film Festival, for cultural leadership and artistic legacy
* Rebeca Calvet, Co-Founder of Club Expats, for global community building
* Gustavo Garagorry, Republican candidate for City of Doral Council, Seat #2, for civic engagement
* Adriana Aiello Ambrugna, Broker & Developer, RealFinity, for excellence in real estate and construction
* Nick Brunzell, CEO & Co-Founder of Archangel Brokers Insurance, for entrepreneurial leadership
* Juliana Hecker, Cultural Promotion Coordinator, Argentine Consulate in Miami, for over 15 years of cultural diplomacy
* Norberto Spangaro, Philanthropist and CEO of MIArgentina, recognized also for his distinguished career as former Latin America Director at Citibank, having retired from banking after achieving major strategic objectives
* Gioconda Salcedo, Venezuelan singer and writer, Director of Fundación Almenar Otero
* Jonathan Amado, Colombian visual artist
* Carina Moffatt, Top Producer, Moffatt Real Estate Group

 Special Recognitions

 Special mentions were awarded to individuals whose leadership strengthens journalism, arts, and civic responsibility:

 * Yvette Franco, President, National Association of Hispanic Journalists – South Florida
* Paula Sánchez Ariza, Colombian muralist invited to paint at Art Basel Wynwood
* Dr. Pedro González Jr., President of the Florida Association of Hispanic Journalists (FAHJ) and university professor

 A Celebration of Hispanic Excellence

 The evening was enriched by artistic expression, meaningful dialogue, and a shared vision of Hispanic leadership. The awards ceremony reaffirmed the importance of creating platforms that elevate Hispanic voices and foster collaboration across sectors.

 Looking Forward

 The Top Entrepreneur Awards 2025 reaffirm Hispanic Target Magazine’s commitment to celebrating those who lead with purpose, creativity, and social responsibility. Surrounded by art, culture, and entrepreneurial excellence, the event stood as a powerful testament to the strength and diversity of the Hispanic community in South Florida.

 Hispanic Target continues to build bridges between talent, opportunity, and visibility—because celebrating Hispanic excellence is not just an event, it is a mission.
