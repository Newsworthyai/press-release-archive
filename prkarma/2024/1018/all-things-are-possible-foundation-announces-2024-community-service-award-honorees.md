# All Things Are Possible Foundation Announces 2024 Community Service Award Honorees

BURLINGTON, NJ / PR Karma / October 18, 2024 10:00

Willingboro, NJ, October 18, 2024. All Things Are Possible  Foundation (ATAP) will host the fourth annual ATAP Community Service Awards, recognizing the contributions and achievements of Burlington County residents and business leaders who help empower our youth and enhance our community.

 “A community must reflect and celebrate the impact so many people and organizations make for the betterment of the community and the sacrifices they make for the greater good,” said Dr. Tiffani Worthy, ATAP Co-Founder and CEO. “This annual event is a wonderful opportunity to do just that, and ATAP encourages the entire community to join us in celebrating and expressing our gratitude.”

 Nominations were accepted and reviewed by the awards committee.

 The 2024 ATAP Kwanzaa Community Service Award Honorees are:

 * Building for the Future: Lori Wilson, Latimani Morgan, and Tatiauna Morgan, Willingboro Recreation Competition Cheerleading
* Courageous Leadership Award: Dr. Felicia Hopson, Burlington County Commissioner Director
* Faith & Trust: Rev. Dr. Danny E. Scotton Sr. & First Lady Linda L. Scotton, Alpha Baptist Church
* Hope & Inspiration: Jasmine Poole, Founder JP Elite and Educator
* Humility & Strength:  Adrain Jones
* Peace & Unity: Donte Milligan, Doane Academy
* Perseverance: Lance Muhammad, The Supreme Sweets Dessert Shop, Nation of Islam Delaware Valley Regional Secretary, School Principal
* Understanding & Cooperation:  Nika Corbett, Curate Noir
* ATAP Scholar: Bellah Jackson, High School Scholar

 All award recipients will be honored at the 2024 ATAP Community Service Awards & Kwanzaa Gala on Friday, December 6, 2024 at The Merion located at 1301 Route 130 Cinnaminson, NJ 08077. Tickets are on sale at Kwanzaa Community Service Awards.

 About All Things Are Possible Foundation: For more than 17 years, ATAP, a 501c3 nonprofit organization, has offered life-changing, developmental programs that uplift and empower students to attain greatness. Our mission is to empower youth and to enhance the community through academics, athletics, arts, and awareness in response to critical youth and community needs. ATAP Co-founder and CEO Dr. Tiffani Worthy is a Willingboro resident, and business and community leader. She is a West Point graduate and decorated combat Veteran who earned a doctorate in education. As a working mother, she was disappointed in the limited choices for academic enrichment and afterschool programs and developed ATAP’s fun learning curriculum to help youth and teens discover what’s possible. Discover more at www.atapfoundation.org.
