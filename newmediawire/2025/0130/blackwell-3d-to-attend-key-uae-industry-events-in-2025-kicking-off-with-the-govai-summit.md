# Blackwell 3D to Attend Key UAE Industry Events in 2025, Kicking Off With the GOVAI Summit

DUBAI, UAE - January 30, 2025 (NEWMEDIAWIRE) - Blackwell 3D Construction Corp. (BDCC), a leader in innovative 3D printing solutions for the construction sector, is thrilled to announce its participation as an attendee in a series of major industry events across the UAE in 2025. These include cutting-edge AI summits, construction conferences, and finance exhibitions. BDCC’s presence at these events is a key part of its strategy to network with leading players and stay at the forefront of the AI and construction technology sectors in the UAE.

 BDCC’s first stop will be the GOVAI Summit, taking place on 13th May 2025 in Abu Dhabi. The summit focuses on the transformative power of Artificial Intelligence in government, aligning with the UAE’s National AI Strategy for 2031. As one of the key events driving discussions around AI and innovation in governance, BDCC is excited to join industry leaders and policymakers in exploring the future of AI in the region.

 "We are extremely excited to attend the GOVAI Summit and connect with the leaders shaping the future of AI and governance in the UAE," said Mohammedsaif Zaveri, CEO of Blackwell 3D. "This summit provides a valuable opportunity to engage with key stakeholders, learn about the latest AI trends, and foster relationships that will help us continue to innovate in the construction sector through 3D printing."

 The GOVAI Summit will feature an impressive lineup of keynote speakers and panelists, including:

 - H.E. Dr. Mohamed Al-Kuwaiti – Head of Cyber Security, UAE Government

 - H.E. Dr. Ohoud Ali Shehail – Director-General, Ajman Digital Government

 - H.E. Eng. Matar Almheiri – Assistant Secretary General, Federal National Council

 - H.E. Mohammad Hassan – Executive Director, National Data & Statistics Sector, Federal Competitiveness & Statistics Centre

 - Major Dr. Eng. Meiaad Alsaadi – Chief AI Officer, Ministry of Interior

 - Mohammad Almansoori – Chief AI Officer, Ministry of Foreign Affairs

 - Saqr Alhemeiri – Chief Innovation Officer, Ministry of Health and Prevention UAE (MOHAP)

 - Tareque Choudhury – Vice President, Technology, Dubai Airports

 - Maya Omeiri – Industry Leader and Innovation Strategist

 BDCC is excited to attend a series of panel discussions, networking sessions, and technology innovation showcases, where the latest advancements in AI and digital transformation will be explored. The summit's agenda includes topics like AI’s role in enhancing public services, economic growth, and building the AI workforce, all of which are highly relevant to BDCC's future plans in the UAE.

 “We’re looking forward to learning from industry experts and forging new partnerships at the summit,” added Mohammedsaif Zaveri. “As the UAE continues to embrace AI in governance and business, we believe that BDCC’s involvement in these discussions will help drive the future of construction and 3D printing innovation in the region.”

 The GOVAI Summit will bring together over 400 attendees, including top decision-makers from government ministries and agencies, AI experts, and technology leaders, providing BDCC with a unique opportunity to build connections that will benefit the company’s future growth in the UAE.

 BDCC's participation at the GOVAI Summit is just the beginning. Throughout 2025, the company will continue to engage with the region’s key industries by attending major AI and construction events, staying committed to its mission of delivering cutting-edge 3D printing solutions for the UAE’s growing infrastructure needs.

 For further information about Blackwell 3D, please visit our corporate website at www.blackwell3d.com or refer to filings available at www.otcmarkets.com and www.sec.gov.

 About Blackwell 3D Corporation

 Blackwell 3D Construction Corp. is focused on developing state-of-the-art 3D technologies for use in the construction industry. Our goal is to be able to use our technology to design 3D printed residential structures and that meet our clients' specific requirements and preferences. In addition, it is our objective to use a large-scale printer and a specialized concrete mixture to print the structural components of these residential structures. Our printing process is expected to be highly automated, allowing us to create intricate designs and details with precision.
