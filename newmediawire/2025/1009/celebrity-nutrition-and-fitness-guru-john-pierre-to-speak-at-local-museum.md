# Celebrity Nutrition and Fitness Guru John Pierre to Speak at Local Museum

![martial arts museum - john pierre 1](https://iprsoftwaremedia.com/26/files/20259/martial%20arts%20museum%20-%20john%20pierre%201.png)

 GLENDALE, CA - October 9, 2025 (NEWMEDIAWIRE) - The Martial Arts History Museum is thrilled to announce a special appearance by renowned nutrition and fitness consultant, John Pierre, on Saturday, October 11, 2025, at 4 p.m. The public is invited to attend this engaging and life-changing free event.

 John Pierre, a personal trainer to celebrities, rock stars, and Fortune 500 CEOs, is celebrated for his unique, science-backed, and practical approach to wellness. With over 35 years of experience, he has developed cutting-edge programs blending modern science with ancient modalities, captivating and entertaining audiences worldwide with his fascinating blend of humor, science, and practicality.

 Attendees will have the opportunity to hear John Pierre discuss his holistic philosophy for lifelong wellness, detailed in his book, The Pillars of Health: Your Foundations for Lifelong Wellness. This framework is built upon four foundational principles: real food, mind stimulation, physical movement, and spiritual philosophy.

 ![martial arts museum - ellen](https://iprsoftwaremedia.com/26/files/20259/martial%20arts%20museum%20-%20ellen.jpg)

 Ellen Degeneres John Pierre's sample talks include "Fine-tuning the Vegan Diet," "The Power of a Plant-Based Diet," and "Veganism - More than a Diet, It’s a Lifestyle." As a pioneer in the brain-building field for the elderly, he created the first programs utilizing a plant-based diet and nutraceuticals over 25 years ago.

 In addition to his work with high-profile clients, John Pierre is the author of two books, cooking DVDs, exercise videos, and numerous online programs. He also leads the nonprofit organization, Living With Harmony, which works to heal, inspire, and transform lives through the education of "PLANET-Based Living."

 “We are excited to host John Pierre and share his wealth of knowledge with our community,” said Michael Matsuda, president of the museum. “His approach to health is inspiring and accessible, and we encourage everyone interested in improving their well-being to join us for this special evening.”

 The Martial Arts History Museum is located at 201 N. Brand Blvd., B100, Glendale, CA 91203. The event is free of charge. Parking is located at 222 N. Orange Blvd. For info, visit MAmuseum.com. For info on John Pierre, visit JohnPierre.com.
