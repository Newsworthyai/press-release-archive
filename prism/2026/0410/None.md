 Desalination Latin America industry facts:

 * Desalination’s global impact: More than 17,000 desalination plants worldwide produce around 107 million cubic meters per day, supplying water to over 300 million people.
* Latin America’s investment momentum: More than $20 billion has been committed to desalination projects across Latin America, reflecting accelerating deployment.
* Chile as the regional anchor market: Chile represents the core of regional investment, with over $18 billion committed, largely driven by copper mining demand.
* Installed desalination capacity in Chile: The country operates 11 desalination plants with a combined capacity of 6,472 liters per second, primarily serving industrial users.
* Mining-driven demand and water mix transformation: Water demand from Chile’s copper mining sector has increased significantly, while seawater now represents more than 30% of total mining water use.
* Regional project pipeline and public-sector push: Latin America has more than 25 desalination projects under development, led by Chile and Peru, with Peru actively promoting six new seawater desalination plants to expand access to drinking water.
