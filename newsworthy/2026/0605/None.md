# Chisos Releases a New Run of the Only Bench-Made Cowboy Boot Built from Wild Texas Alligator

Ahead of rodeo season, Chisos Boot Company is releasing a new run of the Chisos Reserve: Anniversary Edition, perhaps the only small-batch cowboy boot in production built from wild-harvested Texas alligator. The additional units mark one of the few release windows the company opens each year for its rarest boot - and the clearest statement of where this family-owned Austin house sits in the luxury bootmaking market.

 The cowboy boot market divides into three tiers. Work boots stamped out on automated lines for scale anchor the bottom; brands like Ariat lead it, with many manufactured overseas. Fast-fashion mass-production western labels such as Tecovas occupy the middle, borrowing many of the same techniques. At the top is a small group of bench-made heritage makers who build one pair at a time, primarily by hand, on exceptional hides beyond the margin of intermediate brands. Chisos competes in that top tier on construction and materials, not marketing. The Anniversary Edition is the proof.

 [The Anniversary Edition: The Rarest Boot in American Bootmaking](https://chisos.com/pages/anniversary-edition) Each pair is mirror-cut from royal black alligator belly, wild-harvested from the waters of East Texas - several hides hunted personally by founder Will Roman. No two pairs are alike. Each hide carries a signature as distinct as a fingerprint, and none can be replicated. Wild alligator is dense, unruly, and unforgiving; only a handful of master bootmakers in the Guanajuato workshop have the finesse to meet Chisos's tolerances on it, which is why production is small and availability is limited to select release windows.

 The collar carries a hand-tooled, hand-dyed relief of the Chisos Mountains in Big Bend National Park, shaped one strike at a time. Alligator pulls run along either side. The heels are stamped with the shape of Texas. Each pair is pressed with the "Chisos Reserve" interior emblem. The Anniversary Edition retails for $4,950.

 First released in 2020 as the inaugural Chisos Reserve project, it remains the only small-batch cowboy boot exclusively made from wild Texas alligator. The harvest is a conservation model, not a cost to it: regulated wild harvest gives Texas landowners a direct financial reason to protect the wetlands the species depends on. The project is certified by the nonprofit Texan by Nature.

 "Landowners are key to protecting alligator breeding habitat" Roman said. "Using the leather as a model for financing conservation is the reason these animals were able to flourish again - and the reason a boot like this can exist at all."

 The exotic ladder: from Lizard to the Reserve The Anniversary Edition sits at the top of a deliberate exotic range. Its entry point is the Chisos No. 7 Lizard, a black-lizard suit boot that pairs a tight, uniform lizard vamp with the company's heritage magic-black cowhide shaft and signature heel stripe. Lizard is a refined exotic in bootmaking: it moves between a charcoal suit and raw denim without changing register - confident rather than loud. Hand-lasted, Goodyear-welted, and built on a stacked leather heel over a veg-tanned sole, the No. 7 Lizard retails for $945 with other exotics landing at $1295.

 Together the two boots define the range a heritage luxury house is supposed to have: a wearable exotic to begin with, and a once-in-a-lifetime exotic to aspire to. Both are built on the same uncompromising foundation. Neither will ever be mistaken for off-the-shelf.

 Construction: why a Chisos costs what it does The exotic leather catches the eye. The construction underneath justifies the price.

 Every Chisos cowboy boot is bench-made on the same foundation, exotic or not. The heel counter is cut from double-thick vegetable-tanned leather, not the fiberboard or synthetic celastic volume makers use. The outsole and midsole are 10-to-11-iron center-cut veg-tanned leather. The heel is stacked layer by layer and finished with a leather rand (again, no plastic used). Soles are set with both lemonwood pegs - which expand and contract in tandem with the leather - and brass nails for mechanical reinforcement. A conformed steel shank rides between insole and outsole for arch support. The interior is fully lined in leather.

 Every Chisos boot is built to be repaired and rebuilt. Because each pair uses Goodyear welt construction with a leather channel welt, the sole can be replaced as often as the wearer needs - on the assumption the boot core will outlast its first owner. The veg-tanned interior conforms to the foot over time. The exotic leather on top improves with age.

 The one piece of modern engineering Chisos developed in-house is hidden: a removable comfort insole, topped with heritage leather over two layers of semi-organic polymer with impact-dissipating gel pads at the heel and ball. The comfort is engineered, not incidental, but it is the construction, not the cushioning, that defines the boot.

 "We build the way bootmakers built a century ago, because that is what this caliber of boot demands," Roman said. "A Chisos boot is meant to be the last pair a man buys, and the first thing his son inherits."

 The workshop and the maker Every Chisos boot is built at the same family-run workshop in Guanajuato, Mexico, where Roman learned the trade before founding the company in 2019. Heritage leathers are hand-selected from regional dairy-cow hides, tanned in a lead-free process, and chosen in part to reduce demand for juvenile-animal harvesting. Exotics are sourced from the most respected suppliers or harvested by Chisos themselves.

 About Chisos Chisos is a family-owned luxury cowboy boot company based in Austin, Texas, founded in 2019 by native Texan Will Roman and named for the Chisos Mountains in Big Bend National Park. Chisos builds bench-made heritage boots in small batches, using traditional Goodyear welt construction, vegetable-tanned leather components, wild-harvested and hand-selected exotic hides, and a proprietary removable comfort insole. A portion of every sale supports Texas land conservation. Learn more at https://chisos.com.
