# SUCCESS® Announces Weekly Bestseller Lists for September 13, 2024

Dallas, TX (Newsworthy.ai) Tuesday Sep 17, 2024 @ 8:00 AM Central — SUCCESS®, the leading platform for personal and professional growth, today announces its weekly bestseller lists across seven categories: Business, Marketing, Leadership, Entrepreneurship, Money, Personal Development, and General Non-Fiction. This weekly update provides readers with the most current insights into the books shaping success journeys across the nation.

“Our weekly bestseller lists continue to reflect the pulse of what’s resonating with success-minded individuals nationwide,” says Kerrie Lee Brown, vice president of media of SUCCESS® Enterprises and editor-in-chief of SUCCESS® magazine. “This week, we’re seeing some interesting shifts in reader preferences and emerging trends across our categories.”

Notable Trends and Movements From Last Week* Dan Martell’s Buy Back Your Time Shows Strong Performance: This title has climbed to the top of the Business and Entrepreneurship categories, and ranks high in Leadership and Non-Fiction as well, indicating broad appeal across multiple domains.
* Good Energy by Casey Means and Calley Means Dominates Personal Development: This new entry has taken the top spot in the Personal Development category and ranks second in Non-Fiction, suggesting a growing interest in energy management and wellness.
* Consistent Performers: Books like Atomic Habits by James Clear and The Psychology of Money by Morgan Housel continue to maintain strong positions across multiple categories, demonstrating their enduring relevance.
* Emerging Titles: New entries like Supercommunicators by Charles Duhigg and The Anxious Generation by Jonathan Haidt are making their mark across multiple categories, reflecting current interests in communication skills and mental health.
* Shift in Business Focus: The Business category sees a mix of time management (Buy Back Your Time), communication (Supercommunicators), and strategic thinking (48 LAWS OF POWER) at the top, indicating a diverse range of interests among business readers.
* Women’s Leadership: Debuts as a new category this week featuring books focused on women’s leadership like Taking Up Space by Karen S. Lynch and Arrive and Thrive by Susan Brady, Janet Foutty, and Lynn Perry Wooten appear to be gaining traction.
