# Intellum and Talview Partner to Strengthen Skills Validation and Secure Assessments

Atlanta, GA (Newsworthy.ai) Wednesday Aug 13, 2025 @ 7:00 AM US/Eastern — Intellum, the leading enterprise learning platform, today announced a strategic partnership with Talview, a leader in AI‑powered interviewing, proctoring, and assessment technologies. This collaboration empowers organizations to integrate real-world skill validation and secure assessment experiences directly into scalable digital learning programs.

Talview’s GenAI platform—including its flagship “Alvy” AI proctoring agent—provides end-to-end interviewing and remote exam delivery with advanced facial/voice recognition, bias‑mitigating analytics, and secure browser feature. Used in 120 countries and trusted to assess over 10 million candidates worldwide, Talview ensures integrity‑first learning with seamless integration into LMS environments.

“Learners today must not only absorb knowledge, but also demonstrate it under real conditions,” said Dan Hynes, SVP of Partnerships at Intellum. “By embedding Talview’s secure, AI‑powered assessment capabilities into our platform, we’re helping clients measure skills confidently and ensure their programs are built on verified performance, not just content completion.”With this partnership, organizations using Intellum can seamlessly include proctored exams, AI‑driven interviews, and integrity scoring in their learning paths, raising standards for certification, compliance, and customer/partner education. Together, Intellum and Talview deliver learning experiences that are trusted, measurable, and secure at scale.

“At Talview, our mission has always been to enable trust in every learning decision assisted by cutting edge technology,” said Sanjoe Jose, CEO of Talview. “Our partnership with Intellum represents a shared commitment to delivering secure, scalable, and high-integrity learning experiences. By integrating Talview’s Agentic AI proctoring and assessment solutions into Intellum’s robust platform, we’re ensuring that skills are not only taught, but confidently validated—empowering organizations to make decisions with confidence.”
