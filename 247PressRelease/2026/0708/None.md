# LOS ANGELES FAMILIES CELEBRATE THE 250TH ANNIVERSARY OF AMERICA AT THE CHURCH OF SCIENTOLOGY'S STARS AND STRIPES SUMMER FESTIVAL

LOS ANGELES, CA, July 08, 2026 /24-7PressRelease/ -- L. Ron Hubbard Way came alive with music, laughter, and patriotic celebration on Saturday, July 4, as families and community members gathered to commemorate the 250th anniversary of independence for the United States at the Church of Scientology of Los Angeles' Stars and Stripes Summer Festival.  Families from across Los Angeles enjoyed an afternoon filled with carnival games, water slides, and a wide variety of summer activities that kept children laughing and parents joining in the fun.  While many families regularly attend the Church of Scientology of Los Angeles' community festivals, this year's celebration also welcomed many first-time guests, including families new to the neighborhood, newcomers to Los Angeles, and visitors from outside California.  As the sun set, the festivities continued with an energetic concert emceed by renowned American actor, comedian, and voice artist Jim Meskimen and American actress Amrapali Ambegaokar, whose lively stage presence kept the audience entertained throughout the evening. Meskimen delighted the crowd with his signature multi-voice rendition of "America the Beautiful," drawing enthusiastic applause.  The concert showcased the rich diversity of Los Angeles through performances spanning multiple musical styles, from classic country by bluegrass duo Korey Simeone and Patrick Rieger, to soul and gospel by Los Angeles singer-songwriter Prez Blackmon, and vibrant Latin music performed by Puerto Rican singer Luis Adrian.  The celebration took place on L. Ron Hubbard Way. This street was named in honor of author, humanitarian, and Scientology founder L. Ron Hubbard. Mr. Hubbard's humanitarian work has contributed to efforts to combat illiteracy, drug abuse, and criminality in Los Angeles and around the world. Over the years, the street has become a gathering place for community celebrations, cultural festivals, and events that bring together people from all backgrounds.  This year's Stars and Stripes Summer Festival celebrated not only America's 250-year history but also the enduring strength of community, providing families with a safe, welcoming, and festive place to celebrate the values of freedom, unity, and neighborly spirit.  The Church of Scientology of Los Angeles welcomes the community to attend future events throughout the year and continue celebrating the spirit of unity, service, and tradition. All are welcome.  About the Church of Scientology of Los Angeles  The Church of Scientology of Los Angeles is a community hub, hosting numerous events throughout the year, including Easter, the Fourth of July, Halloween, Día de los Muertos, the Holiday Lights Lighting Ceremony, and the popular Candy Cane Lane on the weekend before Christmas. The Church also organizes cultural fairs, open houses, and community forums addressing important social issues affecting the Los Angeles community.  The Church of Scientology of Los Angeles, located at the intersection of Sunset Boulevard and L. Ron Hubbard Way, is featured in Inside a Church of Scientology on the Scientology Network, which was launched by Scientology ecclesiastical leader Mr. David Miscavige in 2018 to commemorate L. Ron Hubbard's birthday.  As an Ideal Scientology Organization, the Church is designed not only to serve Scientologists in their spiritual journey but also to function as a community resource—a meeting place where people of all faiths can collaborate on initiatives that benefit the greater Los Angeles area.  For information on upcoming events, visit the Church of Scientology Los Angeles, Facebook, or Instagram.
