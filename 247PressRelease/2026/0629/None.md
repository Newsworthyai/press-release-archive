# The Best Time to Walk Your Client Through the Numbers Is Before They Fall in Love With the Wrong One

NEW YORK, NY, June 29, 2026 /24-7PressRelease/ -- The moment a client hears a potential settlement figure, they start doing math in their head. They are paying off debt. They are replacing the car. They are covering six months of missed work. By the time you sit down to explain fees, liens, and medical bills, you are not delivering information. You are taking something away.  Segal Law Firm built a free settlement disbursement calculator at www.segal-lawfirm.com so that conversation happens at the beginning of the case, not the end.  Set the Number Before the Number Sets Expectations  Experienced personal injury attorneys know that the gross settlement figure is never what the client keeps. Attorney fees, outstanding medical expenses, health insurance liens, and case costs all come out before a dollar reaches the client's pocket. That is not a surprise to you. But to your client, it can feel like one if they were never shown the full picture upfront.  The calculator gives you a structured way to have that conversation on day one. Before negotiations begin. Before a number gets floated. Before your client has already spent the money in their head. You walk them through the realistic range of outcomes, show them exactly how disbursements work, and build an expectation that reflects reality rather than the best case scenario they read about online.  That is not a difficult conversation. That is client development. And it makes every conversation that follows easier.  The Cases Where Early Clarity Matters Most  The more complex the injury, the wider the gap between gross recovery and net recovery, and the more important it is to get ahead of that gap early. Clients with herniated disks requiring ongoing injections, bulging disc diagnoses with extended recovery timelines, fusion surgeries, or conditions like RSD reflex sympathetic dystrophy are often facing substantial liens and mounting medical expenses that will directly affect their final number.  If those clients have been carrying an inflated expectation for months, the closing table becomes a problem you created at intake by not addressing it. The calculator lets you address it there instead. Show the client the variables. Show them the range. Show them that their attorney is thinking about the full picture from day one, not just the headline number.  That kind of transparency does not undermine confidence in the case. It builds it.  Attorneys Who Set Expectations Early Do Not Manage Disappointment Later  Personal injury attorneys handling construction site accidents, rear end collisions, slip and fall cases, and catastrophic injury claims already carry a full caseload. The legal work is demanding enough without spending the back half of a case managing a client who was never properly oriented at the front.  The attorneys who use this tool have a different kind of client relationship. Their clients understand from the first meeting how a settlement actually works. They know what fees look like. They know what liens do to a recovery. They know their attorney is being straight with them. By the time a real number is on the table, there is no gap between expectation and reality because that gap was closed before it ever had a chance to open.
