# "Shut Out When It Matters Most": FRM² Urges Missouri Legislature to Reform Crisis-Care Laws

KANSAS CITY, KS, July 20, 2026 /24-7PressRelease/ -- Families Rights Matter² (FRM²), a family‑led mental‑health reform initiative, announced today that it has submitted a statewide crisis‑care reform brief to Senator Angela Walton Mosley and Representative Tricia Byrnes, building directly on the priorities outlined in HB 2512 — The Care Before Predictable Harm Act. FRM²'s proposal calls for urgent modernization of Missouri's psychiatric emergency response system. The brief strengthens family involvement by clarifying how crisis communication should operate across two separate federal privacy frameworks:  HIPAA for hospitals, clinics, police, and crisis responders  FERPA for public schools and universities   By distinguishing these legal boundaries, FRM² provides lawmakers with a legally sound statewide model that protects families while respecting federal privacy law.  Representative Byrnes' HB 2512 shifts Missouri toward a predictable‑harm standard, allowing earlier intervention before tragedy occurs. FRM² expands this foundation by proposing unified statewide protocols that ensure families are notified during psychiatric emergencies, repeated crisis holds trigger state‑level review, and Missouri's hospitals, schools, and correctional facilities follow consistent crisis‑care standards.   "Families across Missouri are losing loved ones because they're shut out during psychiatric emergencies," said Leon Shelmire Jr., Founder of FRM². "Senator Mosley and Representative Byrnes understand what's at stake. FRM² builds on the priorities in HB 2512 and scales them into statewide standards that protect every family — ensuring neither HIPAA nor FERPA is used as a shield to keep parents in the dark during a life‑or‑death crisis."  • Emergency Family Notification using HIPAA caregiver exceptions • Cross‑System FERPA Coordination through the Health or Safety Emergency Exception • Mandatory State Intervention after repeated crisis holds • Police & Correctional Stabilization Units — establishing dedicated mental‑health crisis response teams within police departments and clinically supervised stabilization units inside Missouri jails and prisons to ensure individuals in psychiatric crisis receive immediate treatment rather than punitive custody • Workforce Modernization through Medicaid payment reforms • Inpatient Capacity Expansion using a Missouri Section 1115 Waiver  FRM² also submitted a Missouri Case Study Attachment documenting real incidents where families were excluded from crisis decisions or individuals cycled through ERs, schools, and jails without stabilization. Each case demonstrates how FRM²'s dual HIPAA/FERPA clarifying standards could have prevented harm.  "Missouri families deserve a crisis‑care system that protects them instead of leaving them helpless," Shelmire said. "We're encouraged by Senator Mosley's engagement and look forward to working with Representative Byrnes' team to advance solutions that keep our communities safe and save lives."
