# The Ultimate Classic: Rare 1974 BMW 2002tii in Pristine Condition Joins the DFW Car &amp; Toy Museum Collection

FORT WORTH, TX, July 14, 2025 /24-7PressRelease/ -- The DFW Car & Toy Museum is excited to announce the addition of the iconic 1974 BMW 2002tii to its esteemed Ron Sturgeon Collection. A defining model in BMW's history, this meticulously maintained classic showcases the perfect blend of performance, style, and engineering prowess that helped establish BMW as a leader in the automotive world.  The BMW 2002tii was a game-changer in the world of compact sports sedans, and this particular example is no exception. Under the hood, it features an overbored 2.0-liter M10 inline-four engine, paired with a five-speed manual transmission sourced from a BMW 320i. This setup enhances its already spirited driving dynamics, making it a favorite among driving enthusiasts.  The rear Bilstein shocks improve handling, giving the car a firm yet comfortable ride that is well-suited for the road. Inside, the refurbished interior offers a blend of modern and vintage elements, including replacement door panels, a European-market dashboard, and an Alpina shift knob. A Blaupunkt Nevada CD stereo with a body-color faceplate, complemented by an aftermarket sound system, ensures that both driver and passengers enjoy a high-quality audio experience.  At the heart of the driver's experience is the leather-wrapped Alpina steering wheel, which fronts replacement VDO instrumentation, including a 120-mph speedometer, an 8k-rpm tachometer, and a fuel-level gauge. These details not only enhance the car's sporty nature but also make it a joy to drive.  This stunning 1974 BMW 2002tii is more than just a car; it represents an era where BMW refined its identity as the "Ultimate Driving Machine." With its timeless design, high-revving engine, and impeccable craftsmanship, this vehicle is a must-see for classic car enthusiasts and BMW lovers alike.  Visitors to the DFW Car & Toy Museum can now experience this piece of automotive history up close. With over 200 cars and 3,000+ toys on display, the museum offers a unique glimpse into the evolution of automobiles and collectibles. Whether you're a long-time BMW fan or simply appreciate finely crafted automobiles, don't miss the opportunity to see this legendary 1974 BMW 2002tii in person.  About Ron Sturgeon The visionary behind DFW Car & Toy Museum has been a dedicated car collector for over 30 years. His passion for automobiles began in his teenage years when he taught himself to repair Volkswagens, eventually leading to a successful career in the automotive industry. From building one of the largest auto salvage operations in the country to restoring and collecting classic and exotic cars, Ron's love for all things automotive has never wavered.  About DFW Car & Toy Museum  DFW Car and Toy Museum, formerly known as DFW Elite Toy Museum, was founded by Ron Sturgeon in the 1980s collecting automobile toys and cars. Until recently, it was in Haltom City, TX, and its website DFW Elite Toy Museum is still the go-to source for toy and car lovers worldwide. The museum is now located in North Fort Worth at 2550 McMillan Parkway, Fort Worth, TX 76137. (at Meacham and I35w) The new facility is 150,000 square feet with free parking & admission. It promises an entertaining visit. They offer secure climate-controlled car storage and facilities for events, including car clubs, board meetings etc. The museum is also dog friendly and encourages pictures. Hours of operation are Tuesday-Saturday, 9:00 am- 6:00 pm, visit the museum's website at dfwcarandtoymuseum.com.
