# Powerful Documentary, Put The Guns Down: A World Epidemic, by Terry C. Carney, Sr., Selected for Hollywood Weekly Film Festival

LOS ANGELES, CA, November 11, 2025 /24-7PressRelease/ -- Platinum City Golden West Entertainment Films is proud to announce the premiere of its hard-hitting documentary, Put the Guns Down: A World Epidemic, at the Hollywood Weekly Film Festival on Saturday, November 15th. The debut will take place at Warner Brothers Studios.  Executive produced by Ice-T and co-directed by Terry C. Carney, Sr. and Eric Herbert, the film delivers an unflinching exploration of gun violence, with a specific focus on South Central Los Angeles.  Put the Guns Down: A World Epidemic is a powerful, feature-length documentary and the first of a 12-part series. It provides a stark, intimate look at the escalating global crisis of gun violence, humanizing statistics through raw testimonies and unprecedented access. The film explores root causes, such as economic hardship and inadequate mental health care, featuring exclusive interviews with victims, families, community members, and artists like Spice 1, Chilly Chill, and Kokane. More than a film, it's a global rallying cry, aiming to inspire viewers to understand the deeper societal forces behind the violence and catalyze transformative reforms.  The distinguished production team also includes Executive Producers Eugene Ysaguirre, Monae Veronica Chavez, Harold Cheatham, and Juan Villanueva, along with assistant producers Ernest Churchill, Sean E. Sean, Jorge Hinojosa, Cornell Joseph Ward, Tim Crowe, Mack Tompkins, Lawon D. Carney, Sonya A. Carney, Jajuan Bryant, Andre Mitchell, and Devin Mitchell.  More about Terry C. Carney, Sr.: Platinum City Golden West Entertainment Founder Carney was honored with the "Fathers in Hip Hop Fatherhood Acknowledgement Award" for his positive influence and the 2024 TAF Dignitary Award of Supreme Excellence for significant community contributions. The Teachers Appreciation Foundation (TAF) Awards, presented Terry with the 2025 TAF Humanitarian Award.  Born in Denver, Colorado, Carney's early life was challenging, despite his parents' efforts to create a stable home. After relocating near Oklahoma City, he faced the harsh reality of drug-related crime and the incarceration of two brothers for murder. This environment shaped his understanding of life on society's fringes. A move to Los Angeles at age 15 marked a pivotal change. Embracing storytelling, Carney authored his first book and completed his latest film project. His experiences as a father and grandfather influence his narrative style, emphasizing redemption and resilience. The tragic loss of friend and collaborator Nipsey Hussle galvanized his advocacy against gun violence through his art. His collaboration with Cornell Ward and affiliation with Los Angeles Mayor Karen Bass underscore his commitment to diverse voices and critical social issues.
