Insurance Coverage in Rideshare AccidentsOne of the most critical aspects of rideshare accidents is determining insurance coverage. Rideshare companies like Uber and Lyft provide specific policies for their drivers, but the coverage depends on the driver’s activity at the time of the accident.

1. Offline: If the driver is not logged into the app, only their personal insurance applies.
2. App On, No Passenger: Limited coverage from the rideshare company is available.
3. En Route or During a Ride: The rideshare company’s full insurance policy is typically in effect.

Sorting through these layers of coverage can be overwhelming, especially when insurance companies attempt to minimize payouts. This is where an experienced Los Angeles rideshare accident lawyer can make all the difference. Avrek Law Firm helps victims secure the compensation they are entitled to under all relevant policies.

Steps to Take After a Rideshare AccidentAfter a rideshare accident, victims should act quickly and methodically to protect their rights and strengthen their claims. Key steps include:

1. Seek Immediate Medical Attention: Even if injuries seem minor, it’s crucial to get checked by a medical professional.
2. Document the Scene: Take photos, collect witness contact information, and record the rideshare driver’s details.
3. File a Police Report: An official report is often vital for insurance claims.
4. Notify the Rideshare Company: Reporting the accident to Uber or Lyft creates an official record.
5. Consult an Attorney: A knowledgeable LA rideshare accident attorney like Avrek Law Firm can provide invaluable guidance.
