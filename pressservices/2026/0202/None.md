IRS Wage Garnishment vs Illinois Wage Garnishment

In common parlance, the term "wage garnishment" is often used interchangeably for both state creditor garnishments and actions taken by the IRS. In the context of IRS tax liabilities, the more precise term is a wage levy. While it functions similarly to wage garnishment, the governing processes and regulations are federal rather than dictated by Illinois state court rules.

This distinction is crucial as the IRS does not need to initiate a lawsuit in an Illinois court to garnish wages for federal tax debts. Instead, it employs administrative levy authority after providing the necessary notifications and allowing for an appeal.

When Can the IRS Initiate Wage Garnishment in Illinois?

The IRS typically follows a series of notices prior to executing a wage levy. If a taxpayer responds during this notice phase, they can often avert wage garnishment altogether by establishing an installment agreement, securing their account in a protected status, or requesting an appeal.

What Occurs When the IRS Issues a Wage Levy

A wage levy differs from a bank levy. A bank levy typically occurs once and seizes whatever funds are available in the account after a holding period. In contrast, a wage levy is ongoing and repetitive, which can create significant disruption for families attempting to meet rent or mortgage obligations in the Chicago suburbs.

What Your Employer Will Do

Employers generally do not have the discretion to negotiate this process. They are required to comply with the IRS instructions, as failing to honor a levy can expose them to liability. For many individuals in Naperville, the employer's role in this process is primarily administrative, but it still carries a level of stress due to its public nature.

If privacy is a concern, it is crucial to take action before the levy is issued. Once the levy is served, your employer becomes involved in the process.

How Much Can the IRS Withhold From Your Paycheck?

The IRS does not take everything but can withhold a significant amount. The IRS permits an exempt amount based on filing status and dependents, utilizing a standard exemption calculation. Any income above that exempt amount can be subject to withholding.

This means that higher-income earners in Naperville and the surrounding area may experience a much larger portion of their pay being withheld than anticipated, particularly if their exempt amount is relatively small compared to their overall paycheck.

The essential point is that IRS wage levies are often more aggressive than individuals might expect, and they do not function like typical Illinois creditor garnishments.

How to Prevent an IRS Wage Garnishment Before It Begins

If you are still within the CP14, CP501, CP503, or CP504 stages, you typically have options to prevent escalation.

Option 1: Installment Agreement

An installment agreement can often avert levy action, provided that you remain compliant and the agreement is accepted and active. For many taxpayers, this is the quickest way to stop the financial bleeding when garnishment is imminent.

Option 2: Currently Not Collectible Status

If making any payment at this time would hinder your ability to meet essential living expenses, you may qualify for Currently Not Collectible status. While this does not eliminate the debt, it can halt active collection efforts, including wage levies, while your financial situation is assessed.

Option 3: Offer in Compromise

If you cannot pay the full balance and your financial situation justifies it, an Offer in Compromise may allow you to settle the debt for less than the total amount owed. This option requires substantial documentation and is not the quickest solution in an urgent situation, but it can serve as a strong long-term resolution for suitable cases.

Option 4: Collection Due Process Appeal After LT11

If you receive LT11 or Letter 1058, you generally have 30 days to request a Collection Due Process hearing. A timely request typically pauses levy action while the appeal is ongoing. This timeframe represents one of the most significant legal protections available to taxpayers during the wage garnishment process.

How to Stop an IRS Wage Garnishment After It Begins

If your employer has already begun withholding wages, you may still have options to have the levy released. Timing is critical, and the IRS will generally require a clear justification or resolution pathway.

Frequently Asked Questions About IRS Wage Garnishment in Illinois

Can the IRS garnish wages without prior notification?

The IRS is required to provide notice and an opportunity for response before enforcing a levy. In practice, most wage levies follow a series of notices, culminating in a final notice such as LT11 or Letter 1058.

Will the IRS communicate directly with my employer?

Yes, if a wage levy is issued. The levy is served to the employer, who is then obligated to comply.

Does establishing a payment plan halt wage garnishment?

Often, yes, provided that the plan is accepted and you remain compliant. If a levy is already in effect, the IRS may still agree to release it once an agreement is confirmed, but it is important not to assume this will happen automatically.

What if I cannot afford to make any payments at this time?

You may qualify for a hardship-based status such as Currently Not Collectible, depending on your financial situation.
