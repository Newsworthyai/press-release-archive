# Essential Steps for Homeowners Facing AC Breakdowns This Summer

How to Handle AC Failures During Alabama's Hot Summer Months

 SYLACAUGA, United States - March 23, 2026 / Engle Services LLC /

 As summer temperatures soar in Alabama, homeowners often find themselves relying heavily on their air conditioning systems to maintain a comfortable living environment. However, when these systems fail, it can lead to discomfort and even health risks due to prolonged exposure to high heat. Engle Services, a trusted name in HVAC solutions, emphasizes the importance of quick action when faced with an AC breakdown. Homeowners can take several practical steps to troubleshoot their systems before calling for professional help.

The first step homeowners should take when their air conditioning system stops working is to check the thermostat settings. It may seem simple, but sometimes the thermostat can be accidentally adjusted or set to an incorrect mode. Homeowners should ensure that the thermostat is set to "cool" and that the temperature setting is lower than the current room temperature. If the thermostat is battery-operated, checking and replacing the batteries may also resolve the issue.

Next, inspecting and replacing air filters is crucial. Clogged or dirty air filters can restrict airflow, causing the system to work harder and potentially leading to a breakdown. Homeowners should check their filters monthly, especially during peak usage months, and replace them as needed. Engle Services recommends using high-efficiency filters to improve air quality and system performance.

Another important step is to check the circuit breaker. If the air conditioning system is not turning on, it may be due to a tripped circuit breaker. Homeowners should locate their electrical panel and check for any breakers that have flipped to the "off" position. If a breaker has tripped, resetting it may restore power to the AC unit. However, if the breaker continues to trip, it is essential to call a licensed HVAC professional to investigate the underlying issue.

Homeowners should also inspect the outdoor unit for any debris or ice buildup. The outdoor unit is essential for the cooling process, and any obstruction can hinder its performance. Leaves, dirt, and other debris can accumulate around the unit, blocking airflow. Homeowners should clear away any obstructions and ensure that the unit has adequate space for ventilation. Additionally, if ice is present on the unit, it may indicate a refrigerant leak or other issues that require professional attention.

Finally, if these troubleshooting steps do not resolve the issue, it is crucial to call a licensed HVAC professional. Engle Services specializes in emergency AC repair across Alabama, providing rapid-response solutions to ensure homeowners can return to comfort as quickly as possible. "In Alabama, the heat can be relentless, and prolonged exposure can lead to serious health risks, especially for vulnerable populations like the elderly and young children," he explains. "It is vital for homeowners to act quickly and seek professional help to avoid dangerous situations."
