# InvestorNewsBreaks – MAX Power Mining (CSE: MAXX; OTC: MAXXF; FRANKFURT: 89N) Advances Collaboration with PTRC and University of Regina on Canada’s First Natural Hydrogen Drill Program

This article has been disseminated on behalf of MAX Power Mining and may include paid advertising.

 MAX Power Mining (CSE: MAXX; OTC: MAXXF; FRANKFURT: 89N) is strengthening its collaboration with the Petroleum Technology Research Center and the University of Regina through a technical working session today at the U of R Innovation Center, followed by a field visit to the Lawson drill site near Central Butte, Saskatchewan—home to Canada’s first Natural Hydrogen drill program along the 475-km Genesis Trend. Led by Chief Geoscientist Steve Halabura, the session brings together researchers from PTRC, the U of R, the Saskatchewan Geological Survey, and the Colorado School of Mines, including Dr. Yaoguo Li, to deepen understanding of Saskatchewan’s naturally occurring hydrogen systems and identify new research and commercialization opportunities.

 About MAX Power

 MAX Power is an innovative mineral exploration company focused on North America’s shift to decarbonization. The Company is a first mover in the rapidly growing Natural Hydrogen sector where it has built a dominant district scale land position with approximately 1.3 million acres (521,000 hectares) of permits covering prime exploration ground prospective for large volume accumulations of Natural Hydrogen. High priority initial drill target areas have been identified for commencement of drilling in Q4 2025. MAX Power also holds a portfolio of properties in the United States and Canada focused on critical minerals. These properties are highlighted by a 2024 diamond drilling discovery at the Willcox Playa Lithium Project in southeast Arizona.
