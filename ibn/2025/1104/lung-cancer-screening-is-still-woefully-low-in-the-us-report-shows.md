# Lung Cancer Screening is Still Woefully Low in the US, Report Shows

A recent report published by the American Lung Association shows that only a tiny fraction of Americans that are eligible for lung cancer screening undergo the needed screening. This is despite the fact that lung cancer accounts for 20% of all deaths in America attributed to cancer.

 Available data shows that while screening is still very low, it is worth noting that survival rates for lung cancer have registered an uptick and those who exceed 5 years after receiving treatment now stand at 26.6%.
