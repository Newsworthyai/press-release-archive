# Rug Renovating’s Feature on Designing Spaces Highlights the Importance of Artisanal Rug Restoration

The recent feature of Rug Renovating on the award-winning home improvement show Designing Spaces has brought to the forefront the delicate art and science of rug restoration. This segment not only showcased the company’s 125-year legacy but also highlighted the meticulous care and expertise required to restore rugs to their original splendor. From antique treasures to contemporary designs, the process involves a deep understanding of textiles and a commitment to preserving their beauty and history. Laura Iskyan, General Manager at Rug Renovating, shared her pride in the company’s feature, seeing it as a recognition of their dedication to craftsmanship. This exposure is pivotal, not just for Rug Renovating but for the broader appreciation of artisanal skills in an era dominated by mass production. The segment serves as a crucial reminder of the value of expert care in maintaining the integrity and longevity of both family heirlooms and pieces of historical significance.

 The implications of this feature extend beyond the immediate recognition of Rug Renovating’s expertise. It underscores the importance of preserving traditional crafts and the cultural heritage they represent. For homeowners, designers, and collectors, the message is clear: the preservation of these artifacts requires skilled hands and a deep respect for the materials and techniques involved. Rug Renovating’s work ensures that the stories and beauty encapsulated in these rugs are not lost but are instead passed down through generations.

 This segment on Designing Spaces does more than celebrate Rug Renovating’s history; it educates the public on the critical importance of proper rug care and restoration. In a world where the value of craftsmanship is often overlooked, stories like these remind us of the enduring significance of preserving beauty, history, and tradition through expert care. The feature is a testament to the company’s role in setting the gold standard for rug services, while also highlighting the broader cultural and historical value of their work.
