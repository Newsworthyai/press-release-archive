# Interview with Sharon Nevins, Chief Revenue Officer for The Baltimore Banner and Executive Director of The Banner’s Impact Maryland

Sharon Nevins is the Chief Revenue Officer for The Baltimore Banner and Executive Director of The Banner’s Impact Maryland. A highly experienced, results-driven, entrepreneurial sales and marketing executive, Sharon is responsible for the brand strategy and revenue generation for the nonprofit, multi-platform news operation that celebrated its third anniversary in June 2025. Sharon has more than 30 years of experience in multi-disciplinary marketing and media strategy, sales, and brand development, as well as event conception and planning. Sharon’s career has combined a unique blend of driving results for clients and profitability for companies. Sharon began her career on the advertising agency side of the business spending her first decade in account services for a number of Baltimore’s leading advertising agencies. She then spent a decade working internally as lead marketing officer for various organizations before serving in the most senior marketing and sales roles in publishing and media including lead roles for WYPR, Baltimore Magazine and The Baltimore Sun. Sharon graduated from the George Washington University and is fluent in Spanish.

 Interview questions:

 1. The Baltimore Banner has enjoyed amazing growth and huge successes in just its first three years in Baltimore since it launched June of 2022, including quickly becoming the largest newsroom in the state, as well as winning a Pulitzer Prize this past May for its award-winning work in serving as an independent, nonprofit news source for Maryland.
2. You serve several vital roles at The Banner including as Chief Revenue Officer and as Executive Director of the annual Impact Maryland event. First, tell our viewers a little bit about your role as Chief Revenue Officer and how you work with advertisers and other Baltimore Banner partners to help them reach their audiences.
3. One of the important missions of The Banner is to create a series of ongoing events in the community, the most important of which has been Impact Maryland.
4. This October 14th will mark the third annual Impact Maryland thought leadership conference as it brings together top voices and generational leaders to create a powerful dialogue around the most important policies and issues affecting the state.
5. As Executive Director of Impact Maryland, tell us about this year’s event, some of your top sponsors and what attendees can expect throughout the daylong event.
6. In its first two years, Impact Maryland has featured Governor Wes Moore, former U.S. Transportation Secretary Pete Buttigieg, Chief Executive Officer of The Baltimore Orioles David Rubenstein, and many of our region’s leading CEOs. Who are some of your featured speakers and panelists for this year?
7. You tackle many top pressing topics and creative solutions impacting our region each year at Impact Maryland with some of the top CEOs sharing their vision for the future of Maryland… what are some of the topics you will be discussing this year?
8. This event is a great way for attendees to grow their network and strengthen their connections with leaders from various industries across the region. Talk a little bit about those opportunities throughout the day including networking opportunities, lunch, and the cocktail celebration to wrap up the day.
9. You must be proud of how quickly Impact Maryland has built a reputation as the top annual business event in Baltimore, having won best event by the MDDC Press Association for the past two years.
10. How can our viewers learn more about Impact Maryland, plan to attend and purchase tickets online?
11. Before we wrap-up, on a separate note, The Baltimore Banner also recently launched its free monthly Banner Book Club. Tell our viewers about the Banner Book Club, and how they can join to become part of the Banner community.
