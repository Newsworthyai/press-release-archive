# Nan Thai Buckhead Hosts Collaborative Dinner with Chef Joey Ward

One-Night-Only Charity Event Benefits CURE Childhood Cancer on Aug. 26

 WHAT:

 Nan Thai Buckhead chef DeeDee Niyomkul is teaming up with MICHELIN-recommended chef Joey Ward (pictured below) of Southern Belle + Georgia Boy for a one-night-only dinner on Tuesday, Aug. 26. This exclusive dinner experience will showcase the bold flavors and refined techniques of chef Niyomkul’s contemporary Thai cuisine and chef Ward’s ability to capture the tastes of his home state served in a style that Ward calls “authentically Atlanta.”

 The four-course dinner includes wine pairings and offers seatings at 6 and 8 p.m. The cost is $250 per person, inclusive of tax and gratuity. Reservations can be made on OpenTable. A portion of the proceeds will benefit CURE Childhood Cancer, leading into September’s Childhood Cancer Awareness Month.

 About Nan Thai Buckhead

 Nan Thai Buckhead, led by executive chef and owner DeeDee Niyomkul, is located at 3050 Peachtree Road, Suite 8, Atlanta, 30305. Nan Thai Buckhead offers a refined Thai dining experience blending cherished traditional dishes with chef DeeDee’s own modern culinary creations in a chic, relaxed atmosphere. Lunch is served Monday-Friday 11:30 a.m.-2:30 p.m. Dinner is served Monday-Thursday 5-10 p.m. and Friday and Saturday 5-11 p.m. The restaurant is closed on Sundays. Reservations are available on OpenTable or by calling 404-464-7980. Visit nanthai.com for more information.

 About CURE Childhood Cancer

 Founded in 1975, Atlanta, Georgia-based CURE Childhood Cancer is dedicated to conquering childhood cancer through funding targeted research while supporting patients and their families. With cancer as the second leading cause of death in children, CURE dedicate more than $5.7 million in funding to specific research projects aimed at curing cancers that affect children. Through innovative programming, CURE Childhood Cancer also provides crisis-oriented support to patients and their families, addressing their most critical and urgent needs. For more information visit curechildhoodcancer.org.
